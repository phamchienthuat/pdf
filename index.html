DAY 1

Welcome to Hanoi

Meal provided: Dinner (Indian food)


Welcome to Hanoi. After collecting your luggage, you will meet your driver and guide to begin your tour!

1

Ho Chi Minh Complex

Visit the Ho Chi Minh Complex, including Ba Dinh Square, the Presidential Palace, Ho Chi Minh’s Stilt House, and Ho Chi Minh Museum (mausoleum viewable from outside). Nearby is the iconic One Pillar Pagoda, built on a single stone pillar and designed as a lotus blossom, symbolizing purity in Buddhism.

2

Temple of Literature

Visit the Temple of Literature (Vietnamese name is Quoc Tu Giam) – The Vietnam’s first University, which was built in 1070 in honor of Confucius, his followers, and Chu Van An, a moral figure in Vietnamese education.

3

Hanoi Old Quarter

Take a cyclo in Hanoi Old Quarter – 36 streets and guilds to discover the ancient feature of Hanoi and daily life. Stop to visit Ngoc Son Temple, located at the northern end of Sword Lake (aka Hoan Kiem Lake). From the shore, walking on the Welcoming Morning Sunlight Bridge leads to the island.

Ha Long Bay

DAY 4

Ho Chi Minh City Tour - Cu Chi Tunnels

Meal provided: Breakfast, local lunch, Indian dinner


Explore the historical landmarks of Ho Chi Minh City

1

Cu Chi Tunnels

Tunnels After breakfast, visit the historic Cu Chi Tunnels, an extensive underground network built from 1948 to support resistance against the French and later expanded during the Vietnam War. This underground city included living quarters, kitchens, hospitals, and weapon factories, housing up to 10,000 people. Heavily bombed during the war, two sections of the tunnels have been open to visitors since 1988

2

Ho Chi Minh City Center

Visit City Hall, the Ho Chi Minh Monument, and the Central Post Office, a neoclassical masterpiece designed by Gustave Eiffel during French Indochina. Nearby, admire the Notre Dame Cathedral, built by French colonists between 1863 and 1880, featuring twin bell towers rising 58 meters tall.

Thien Hau Temple

Our next destination is Thien Hau pagoda which is dedicated to Thien Hau, the Lady of the Sea. This temple is located right on busy Nguyen Trai Street in the Cho Lon (China town)

3

Ben Thanh Market

Pay a visit at Ben Thanh market, one of the earliest surviving structures in Saigon and one of symbols of Hochiminh City, popular with tourists seeking local handicrafts, textiles, ao dai, and souvenirs, as well as local cuisine

Stay overnight in Saigon

DAY 5

Ho Chi Minh - Noon Flight to Siem Reap

Meal provided: Breakfast, Indian lunch, Indian dinner


Begin your day by exploring historical sites in Ho Chi Minh City before transferring to Siem Reap to start your journey to the wonders of Angkor

1

Reunification Palace

After breakfast, enjoy the sightseeing to Reunification Palace formerly known as Independence Palace, built on the site of the former Norodom Palace, a landmark in Hochiminh City.

2

War Remnants Museum

Our next stop is the War Remnants Museum. Operated by the Vietnamese government, an incipient form of museum opened on September 4, 1975, as the "Exhibition House for US and Puppet Crimes, located in the premises of the former United States Information Agency building

3

Siem Reap, Cambodia

Upon arrival, clear Immigration and Customs and start our tour to visit the South Gate (with its huge statues depicting the churning of the ocean of milk), the ancient capital of Angkor Thom (12th century), Bayon Temple (unique for its 54 towers decorated with over 200 smiling faces of Avolokitesvara), the Royal Enclosure, Phimeanakas, the Elephants Terrace and the Terrace of the Leper King. Sunset at Angkor Wat

Overnight in Siem Reap

Siem Reap - Kulen National Park - 1000 Linga River and Reclining Buddha

Discover Kulen National Park’s pristine natural beauty, including the River of a Thousand Lingas and the Reclining Buddha

1

Kulen National Park

After breakfast, head to Kulen National Park, 55km from Siem Reap, passing scenic countryside with rice paddies and Khmer traditional houses. Visit the 9th-century 1000 Lingas carved on the riverbed, the reclining Buddha, and a 23-meter waterfall. Enjoy a picnic lunch on-site, relax by the waterfall and jungle, and then return to Siem Reap

Slem Reap - Departure

Morning visit to the renowned Angkor Wat

1

Angkor Wat

In the morning, visit the iconic Angkor Wat, the largest temple complex in Angkor, spanning 81 hectares.
